# AIVMT — Project Plan

> **Title (working):** AIVMT ("AI Virtual Medical Teacher") — a self-hosted **open-weight-model competency-scoring pipeline** for clinical communication, validated against blinded faculty, with an **ultra-low-cost embodied voice standardized patient** on an ESP32-S3 device as a deployment vehicle.
> **Target:** Research Article, *npj Digital Medicine* collection **"Transforming Medical Education through Artificial Intelligence."**
> **Hard deadline:** **2026-07-24** (~6.5 weeks from today, 2026-06-09; near-zero slack).
> **Locked plan: Tier B-prime (IRB-light validity baseline).** The **baseline deliverable is SQ1 (local-model scoring validity, on IRB-light archival/instructor-acted transcripts) + SQ3 (cost/equity)** — firmware-independent. SQ2 (embodiment) and the full firmware MVP are an **additive stretch**, not a planned scope that gets cut under pressure. Tier C (Perspective) is the fallback if SQ1 fails its pilot gate. Tier A (full n≈90–120 RCT) is **out of the active plan** — future work / next submission window only.
>
> **Honesty note up front:** the device runs **stock xiaozhi firmware**; the firmware we would build is **apparatus, not a contribution**. The primary scientific result does not yet exist — the phase that computes it is a `NotImplementedError` (`harness/registry.py:69`) fed by a synthetic fixture. This plan is structured so the paper can be delivered **without** the device and **without** prospective student recruitment.

## 2. Goal (the paper + collection alignment + deadline)

**In one sentence:** Produce a publishable **Research Article for *npj Digital Medicine*'s collection "Transforming Medical Education through Artificial Intelligence"** (deadline **2026-07-24**) establishing the first **faculty-valid automated competency scoring on a LOCAL open-weight LLM**, with a cost/edge/equity analysis, and — as a stretch — an embodiment test behind a sub-$20 voice device, deployable where human SPs and cloud LLMs are not.

**What the collection requires (calibrated from `plan/protocol-v0.1.md` §14 and same-journal peers):**
- **Accepted article types:** Research Articles, Perspectives, systematic reviews (Open Access).
- **Empirical bar (RCT framing):** recent npj DM med-ed RCTs run **n≈88–111** (misinformation RCT n=111; 3-arm VR-bias RCT n=88, d=0.67, p=0.010). An n=40 AI-learning RCT "lands in Frontiers, not npj DM." **Our own protocol §14 (line 145) explicitly concludes the *RCT* path "must scale to ~n80–120, multi-arm … materially heavier than v0.1's n≈30–40."**
- **The unresolved tension this plan must own (Critique #4):** the validity reframe — "a *validity* study is judged by **encounters × raters × ICC precision** (cf. AMTES n=31 / 93 sessions), not student n" — is **in direct tension with our own protocol §14's RCT bar.** These cannot both be cited as settled. **Resolution adopted here:** the protocol §14 "n80–120" implication is the bar for an *RCT/effectiveness* claim (SQ2-style). It does **not** govern a *validity* claim (SQ1), which is the baseline deliverable. We therefore **do not** sell SQ1 as an underpowered RCT; we sell it as a validation study whose unit of evidence is the scored encounter. **R4's caveat ("only if reviewers accept it") is propagated into the tier decision, not buried:** if a reviewer treats SQ1 as an RCT, the paper is a validity study + Perspective-grade cost argument, not a failed RCT. Protocol §14's "Implication" sentence will be **edited** to scope its n80–120 claim to the RCT/effectiveness path explicitly, so the prereg pointer is not self-contradictory.
- **Named, validated instruments** (not ad-hoc rubrics): **SEGUE** (locked; 25 items, 5 domains), Calgary-Cambridge as alternative, a named history-taking checklist. **MaSP / SUS / CUQ are SQ2-only** (see §3).
- **Effect sizes + 95% CIs**; an explicit **fairness / validity / equity / patient-confidentiality lens** is invited.

**What the baseline (SQ1 + SQ3) submission must concretely deliver:** a blinded SQ1 validity study on **IRB-light transcripts** (each scored by the system AND ≥2 blinded faculty; ICC(2,k) + 95% CI, weighted κ, Bland–Altman, faculty inter-rater ICC ceiling); enough scored encounters for ICC precision **at the realistic ICC, not the hoped-for one** (see §3 / R-numbers); named validated instruments; an equity/cost/offline analysis **including server amortization** (BOM built in P0); and **pre-registration (with §11 filled) + harness reproducibility** (every manuscript number regenerated by `python -m harness.evidence_table`, with `python -m harness.run_all` exiting 0).

**Stretch (SQ2) adds:** a controlled within-subjects embodiment design (device vs same-model screen), mixed-effects + effect sizes/CIs, MaSP/SUS/CUQ usability instruments, and the firmware MVP that makes it testable.

## 3. Contribution & novelty (SQ1 primary / SQ2 / SQ3)

**Contribution.** The first standardized-patient **competency-scoring** pipeline validated against blinded faculty while running **entirely on a single self-hosted open-weight LLM**, plus a cost/equity analysis, and (stretch) a controlled same-brain embodiment test behind a sub-$20 ESP32-S3 voice device.

**Novelty verdict (locked literature scan): REAL gap, partial per-axis → the contribution is the COMBINATION + controlled tests, never any single component.** Lead with the *triad/frontier*, never a bare "first" — this survives partial scooping.

## 6. Risk register & GO/NO-GO gates

> **Headline judgment:** The dominant risk is **not** the timeline — it is that **SQ1 (the primary claim) rests on an unbenchmarked assumption** (a ~7–14B local model reaching faculty ICC≥0.75) while no frozen candidate has ever run and the phase that computes the headline number is `raise NotImplementedError`. The two paper-deciding assumptions — **(a) can a local model score at faculty level (R1/R6); (b) does an IRB-light, real-transcript data path exist in time (R2/R4)** — are both unconfirmed. **Neither requires students, faculty, or finished firmware to test cheaply this week.** Correct sequence: the R1+R6 synthetic/pilot test (2 days, $0) and the R2/R4 collaborator emails (this week), in parallel — **before another hour of firmware.**

### R1 — SQ1 scientific risk: local model may not reach ICC≥0.75 vs faculty (cloud bar ≈0.92) — **CRITICAL** (High / Fatal to primary)
The wedge is "first faculty-valid scoring on a local open model." Current scorers are thin (raw 0–1 floats, one-line prompt, no anchors/few-shot/item decomposition) — plausibly ICC 0.3–0.6 (below 0.75), pre-registering a null. **Compounding:** zero frozen candidates have run; the W2 benchmark starts from scratch on four un-downloaded models, and the only proven model (`gpt-oss:20b`) is off-prereg.
- **Mitigation:** Rebuild scorers to rubric-decomposed, few-shot, item-level SEGUE (anchors per item) — highest-leverage, hardware-free move. Run `benchmark.py` on 20–30 expert-ordered synthetic transcripts of varying quality NOW. Reconcile gpt-oss:20b vs the frozen list before any real data. Recompute precision/non-inferiority at the gate ICC (0.6–0.75), not 0.85 (Critique #8); drop non-inferiority-vs-0.92 unless the pilot clears ~0.80.
- **GO/NO-GO (this week, ~2 days, $0):** Best candidate reaches **ICC(2,1) ≥ 0.6 against the synthetic gold ordering.** If no open model clears 0.6 on clean synthetic data, it won't clear 0.75 on noisy real ASR → **pivot to Tier C now.**

### R2 — IRB slack is NEGATIVE for a prospective student arm; make IRB-light the primary path — **CRITICAL** (Med-High / Fatal to human-subjects results)
A new device recording student-encounter audio + faculty rating of identifiable performance is not obviously covered by a teaching umbrella; an amendment (weeks) or full review (months) is common. With data collection at W3 (Jun 23) and a ~10-day window to "written coverage by Jun 13," **this is negative slack, not "~2 weeks" (Critique #9).**
- **Mitigation:** **Make the IRB-light path primary, not contingent:** SQ1 validity on **de-identified archival OR instructor-acted transcripts** scored by faculty (commonly exempt / non-human-subjects).
