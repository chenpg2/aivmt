## Central claim (one sentence) — UPDATED 2026-06-10 (user-locked headline: validity–cost frontier)
**How small and cheap a model can still score clinical history-taking at faculty level?** We map
the first **validity–cost frontier** for automated standardized-patient competency scoring —
showing that a local open-weight model at ~XX size reaches faculty-level agreement (ICC ≥ 0.75)
at ≈$0 marginal cost per encounter, offline and data-local — which makes SP practice deployable
where human SPs and cloud LLMs are not. (The device is the deployment vehicle, not the claim.)
Framing rationale: "AI-SP + AI-teacher" as a combo has prior art (Voigt 2025, AMTES, Sun 2026 —
all cloud); the unanswered question we own is the frontier + local-model validity + embodiment.

## Sub-questions (each = a paper result)
| SQ | Question | Hypothesis | Evidence tier | Placement |
|----|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **SQ1** (primary) | **Validity–cost frontier:** at what model size/cost does automated scoring reach faculty-level agreement (and where does it collapse)? | H1 | Inferential (ICC vs faculty, per model tier) | Main |
| **SQ2** | Does **physical voice embodiment** change engagement/realism vs a same-model screen? | H2 | Inferential (RCT-style, mixed-effects) | Main |
| **SQ3** | Total cost / offline capability vs cloud-LLM and human-SP baselines | H3 | Descriptive/analytic | Main |

Closest prior art to beat (lit scan): AMTES (cloud ICC 0.92–0.98), Borg (\$30k robot + cloud),
2026 review ("no offline/edge SP exists"). Our wedge = the **combination + validation on cheap edge**.
